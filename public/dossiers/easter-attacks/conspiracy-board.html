The Rajapaksa Network

Gotabaya Rajapaksa

Former President (2019–2022) • Former Defence Secretary (2005–2015)

Central figure in the conspiracy allegations. Declared presidential candidacy just 2 days after the bombings. Won in a landslide on a national security platform. Immediately dismantled the CID investigation upon taking office. Promoted Sallay to head SIS. Denies all involvement, calling allegations "absurd." Fled the country during 2022 protests.

Promoted Sallay to SIS Funded TMVP via DMI Alleged orchestrator Under investigation

Mahinda Rajapaksa

Former President (2005–2015) • Family patriarch

Founded the Rajapaksa political dynasty. Under his presidency, the TMVP was integrated as a paramilitary ally. The DMI-to-TMVP funding pipeline of Rs. 3.5M/month was established during his tenure. Not directly named in Easter attack conspiracy but heads the political network that benefited.

Family patriarch TMVP patronage BBS political alignment

Basil Rajapaksa

Former Finance Minister • SLPP founder • Political strategist

The "backroom operator" of the Rajapaksa machine. Founded the SLPP party. Managed the family's political comeback strategy. While not directly named in Easter attack allegations, he was the strategic brain behind the Rajapaksa return to power.

SLPP strategist

Mahindananda Aluthgamage

Pro-Rajapaksa MP • SLPP

Public defender of the Rajapaksa position. Argued in Parliament that Gotabaya had no motive for the attacks because SLPP was already winning local elections. Represents the public-facing Rajapaksa defence strategy.

Rajapaksa defender

Intelligence & Military Apparatus

Suresh Sallay (Ret. Maj. Gen.) — ARRESTED

Former SIS Director • Former DMI officer • Arrested 25 Feb 2026

The pivotal figure. Served in the military intelligence unit that funded the NTJ. Whistleblower alleges he met NTJ bombers in 2018 to plan "national insecurity." Promoted to head SIS after Rajapaksa's victory. Dismissed by AKD in Oct 2024. Now arrested under PTA for conspiracy and aiding and abetting the Easter attacks. Previously removed from a posting for links to military-backed gangs in Jaffna. Denies all charges.

Promoted by Gotabaya DMI unit funded NTJ Alleged met NTJ bombers Exposed by Channel 4 Arrested by CID

Pillayan (Sivanesathurai Chandrakanthan)

TMVP Leader • Former Chief Minister, Eastern Province

Led the TMVP paramilitary group, a Rajapaksa-allied Tamil faction. Received Rs. 3.5M/month from military intelligence. His aide Maulana alleges he directed the facilitation of the Sallay-NTJ meeting. Arrested by CID; linked to Easter attacks by Public Security Minister. Has been implicated in abductions, disappearances, and political killings. Denies all Easter attack involvement.

Rs.3.5M/month from DMI Rajapaksa ally Maulana was his aide Eastern Province operations

Directorate of Military Intelligence (DMI)

Institutional actor

The military intelligence arm that allegedly funded the NTJ to propagate fundamentalist ideology in the east. Withheld information from CID about NTJ operatives. Prevented CID from detaining key individuals. The "Sonic Sonic" dossier shows they were tracking NTJ but did not act to prevent the attacks.

Funded NTJ (confirmed) Obstructed CID Suppressed Vavunathivu reports

Nilantha Jayawardena

Former State Intelligence Services Chief

Headed SIS before Sallay's appointment. Fined Rs. 75M by Supreme Court for failure to prevent the attacks. Named as one of the officials who failed to act on intelligence warnings.

SIS failure SC fined Rs.75M

NTJ / Terrorist Network

Zahran Hashim †

NTJ Founder & Leader • Died 26 Apr 2019

Born 1985 in Kattankudy. Founded NTJ ~2014 after splitting from SLTJ. Salafi jihadist who pledged allegiance to ISIS. Preached violent extremism from 2016. Led attacks on Sufi mosques. Masterminded the Easter Sunday bombings. Died detonating a suicide vest during a police raid. His group was tracked by intelligence but warnings were ignored or suppressed.

Founded NTJ ISIS allegiance DMI funded his group Alleged met Sallay

Naufer Moulavi

Alleged mastermind (contested) • In remand custody

Born 1978 in Kattankudy. Sri Lankan government named him as the mastermind. Connected Zahran with ISIS. However, the FBI identified Zahran (not Naufer) as the mastermind. The truth remains contested. Currently in remand.

ISIS connector NTJ leadership FBI disputes govt claim

Rilwan Hashim †

Bomb-maker • Zahran's brother • Died in Sainthamaruthu raid

Built the explosives alongside Mohammad Hasthoon. May have travelled to Turkey for training. Lost fingers in a 2018 explosives accident. Carried out a suicide bombing during the post-attack raids.

Bomb-makerZahran's brother

Other NTJ Operatives

Bombers & support network

Including Ibrahim Ilham, Muhammadu Hasthoon, Ahmed Muath, Ahmed Milhan, Zainee Hashim. Held training camps from Nov 2017 to Dec 2018 across Trincomalee, Mawanella and other locations. 25 suspects total indicted in Sri Lanka's High Court.

Current Government (NPP / AKD)

Anura Kumara Dissanayake (AKD)

President of Sri Lanka (since Sep 2024) • NPP/JVP leader

Won presidency promising Easter attacks prosecutions. Dismissed Sallay in Oct 2024. NPP holds 2/3 parliamentary majority (159/225 seats). Announced independent investigation on 6th anniversary. Criticised by HRW for slow progress through mid-2025, but Sallay arrest fulfils key promise.

Dismissed Sallay Ordered prosecution Under pressure from Cardinal

Shani Abeysekara

CID Director (2017–2019, reinstated Jul 2025)

The investigator who was punished for investigating. Established links between intelligence and NTJ. Transferred within days of Rajapaksa taking office. Arrested on allegedly fabricated charges Jul 2020. Spent 10 months in prison. Assassination plot uncovered 2023. Reinstated Jul 2025. Now leads the renewed investigation. Received INTERPOL commendation.

Leads CID investigation Exposed intel-NTJ links Targeted by Rajapaksa govt

Victims & Advocates

Cardinal Malcolm Ranjith

Archbishop of Colombo • Chief victims' advocate

Lost parishioners in 3 church bombings. Alleges a "grand political plot." Demands independent prosecutor. Took case to UNHRC in Geneva. Former Rajapaksa ally turned fierce critic. Prevented anti-Muslim retaliatory violence. Faces organised campaign of insults and harassment. The most powerful non-governmental voice in this saga.

Took case to UNHRC Demands independent prosecutor Pressures AKD govt Former Rajapaksa ally, now critic

Muslim Community of Sri Lanka (~9.7% of population)

Caught between stigmatisation and the conspiracy narrative

Muslim leaders like Hilmy Ahamed warned authorities about Zahran for years. Mainstream Muslim organisations condemned NTJ. Community suffered collective punishment through anti-Muslim riots, forced cremations, discrimination. If state conspiracy is proven, they are vindicated; if not, stigma persists.

Targeted by BBS Warned about NTJ NTJ emerged from their community

International Actors

India / RAW (Research and Analysis Wing)

Indian intelligence service

Provided 3 specific warnings: April 4 (two weeks before), the night before, and one hour before the attacks. Named Zahran Hashim specifically and identified targets as "popular Catholic Churches and the Indian High Commission." Intelligence came from interrogation of a suspected ISIL recruit in India. Discovered IS cells in Tamil Nadu and Kerala with NTJ contacts. Warnings were catastrophically ignored by Sri Lankan authorities.

3 warnings ignored Named Zahran South India IS cell monitoring

ISIS / Islamic State

Global jihadist organisation

Claimed responsibility via Amaq agency 2 days after attacks. Published video of bombers pledging allegiance to al-Baghdadi. Zahran attempted to establish "Willayath-As-Seylani" (Province of Ceylon) as an IS province. However, investigators found no direct foreign operational link. The connection was ideological and inspirational rather than command-and-control.

Ideological patron Claimed responsibility No operational link found

US Department of Justice / FBI

US federal agencies

Charged 3 Sri Lankans in 2021 for supporting the attacks (5 US nationals killed). FBI affidavit identified Zahran as "the mastermind" — contradicting Sri Lankan government claims about Naufer Moulavi. Provided forensic and investigative assistance.

Charged 3 Sri Lankans Identified Zahran as mastermind

UN Human Rights Council (UNHRC)

Geneva-based international body

Asked Sri Lanka to publish withheld parts of previous inquiry reports. Received testimony from Cardinal Ranjith and civil society. European intelligence agencies treating Maulana's testimony as credible. Provides the "international pressure" lever that domestic actors use.

Demands report disclosure Receives Cardinal's advocacy

South Indian IS Cells (Tamil Nadu / Kerala)

Regional jihadist network

Contacts between Zahran and IS followers in south India discovered by Indian intelligence. A Sri Lankan software engineer monitored since 2016 for IS links. Zahran may have received training or ideological reinforcement through these networks. The regional dimension of the NTJ's radicalisation is often overlooked.

NTJ contacts Monitored by RAW

Media, Whistleblowers & Evidence

Hanzeer Azad Maulana

Key whistleblower • Former Pillayan aide • In exile (Switzerland)

The explosive primary witness. Former coordinating secretary and spokesman for Pillayan (TMVP). Claims he brokered the 2018 meeting between Sallay and NTJ members at Pillayan's direction. Says Sallay told him "national insecurity" was needed to return Rajapaksas to power. Recognised bombers from CCTV footage. Claims Sallay called him on Easter morning to collect a bomber from the Taj Samudra hotel. Now in Switzerland. Testified to European intelligence agencies and UN. Willing to testify at international investigation. His credibility is disputed — critics note he fled only after Rajapaksa regime collapsed and was implicated in financial crimes.

Key whistleblower Testified to Channel 4 Was Pillayan's aide Alleges brokered Sallay-NTJ meeting In exile, testified to European intel

Channel 4 (UK)

British broadcaster • "Dispatches" documentary (Sep 2023)

Aired the documentary "Sri Lanka's Easter Bombings" that triggered the current investigation trajectory. Featured Maulana and a second unnamed senior government official. Obtained documents showing false leads by military intelligence and Indian intelligence warnings. Led to government committee appointment and ultimately to the current prosecutions.

Documentary catalyst Obtained intelligence documents

The Missing Evidence

Two committee reports + SIS infiltrator reports

Report 1: Channel 4 verification committee findings. Report 2: Judge Alwis committee on pre-attack intelligence. Both disappeared before Wickremesinghe left office. Multiple SIS infiltrator reports from within the NTJ have also vanished from CID files. These missing documents are potentially the most critical evidence in the entire case.

Disappeared under Wickremesinghe CID investigating disappearances

Sinhala-Buddhist Nationalist Groups

Bodu Bala Sena (BBS)

Ultra-nationalist Buddhist organisation (founded 2012)

Led anti-Muslim campaigns since 2012. Promoted conspiracy theories about Muslims. Helped create the environment of Islamophobia that made the Easter attacks so politically exploitable. Post-attack, amplified anti-Muslim violence. Political alignment with Rajapaksa network. Reduced influence under current NPP government.

Anti-Muslim campaigns Rajapaksa-aligned

Galagodaatte Gnanasara Thero

BBS General Secretary • Buddhist monk

Called for "stoning Muslims to death" after Easter attacks. Promoted fabricated "sterilisation medicine" conspiracy. Key voice in anti-Muslim mobilisation. Has been arrested and jailed but repeatedly released and pardoned by Rajapaksa governments. Represents the nexus of religious extremism and political power.

Incited anti-Muslim violence Pardoned by Rajapaksas

Former Sirisena Government (Negligence Cluster)

Maithripala Sirisena

Former President (2015–2019) • Also Defence Minister & Commander-in-Chief

Failed to call regular National Security Council meetings. Communication breakdown with PM Wickremesinghe meant intelligence warnings were not actioned. Supreme Court fined him Rs. 100M personally. His own presidential inquiry found him guilty. Made controversial claim that an "Indian diplomat admitted Delhi's involvement" (widely disputed). Represents the negligence pillar of the failure.

SC fined Rs.100M Failed to act on RAW warnings Made claims against India

Hemasiri Fernando, Pujith Jayasundara, Sisira Mendis

Former Defence Secretary, Police Chief, NIS Chief respectively

All named by the Supreme Court for failing to prevent the attacks. Fined Rs. 50M, Rs. 75M, and Rs. 10M respectively from personal funds. Represent the institutional failure at the highest levels of security and defence apparatus.

SC personal fines Institutional failure

Ranil Wickremesinghe

Former PM (2015–2019) • Former President (2022–2024)

PM during the attacks; communication rift with Sirisena blamed for intelligence failure. Later became President (2022–2024). Two committee reports disappeared before he left office. Appointed committee to investigate Channel 4 claims but results were not published. Accused of continuing cover-up by Cardinal Ranjith.
